Depression

A mood disorder that causes severe symptoms that affect how you feel, think and handle daily activities such as sleeping, eating or woking.

Symptoms must be present for at least 2 weeks for depression to be diagnosed:

  • Persistent sad, anxious, or empty mood
  • Feelings of hopelessness, or pessimism
  • Irritability
  • Feelings of guilt, worthlessness, or helplessness
  • Loss of interest or pleasure in hobbies and activities
  • Less energy or fatigue
  • Moving or talking more slowly
  • Feeling restless or having trouble sitting still
  • Difficulty concentrating, remembering, or making decisions
  • Difficulty sleeping, early-morning awakening, or oversleeping
  • Appetite and/or weight changes

Anxiety

The fear and anxiety can cause significant problems in areas of their life, such as social interactions, school, and work.

  • Feeling restless, wound-up, or on-edge
  • Being easily fatigued
  • Having difficulty concentrating; mind going blank
  • Being irritable
  • Having muscle tension
  • Difficulty controlling feelings of worry
  • Having sleep problems, such as difficulty falling or staying asleep, restlessness, or unsatisfying sleep

Bipolar Disorder

A disorder that causes very intense and unmanageble mood swings between manic episodes or depressive episodes that can last for weeks at a time. This causes many flunctuations in energy level which can cause day-to-day life a living hell.

During a manic episode:

  • Feel very up, high, or elated
  • Have a lot of energy
  • Have increased activity levels
  • Become more active and hyper than usual

During a depressive episode:

  • Have increased activity levels
  • Have very little to no energy
  • Have trouble sleeping, they may sleep too little or too much
  • Feel like they can't enjoy anything

Obcessive Compulsive Disorder(OCD)

Having uncontrollable reoccuring thoughts that lead them to do a certain action repetitively, it is chronic but treatments can help severity. These thoughts can also cause immense amont of stress and anxiety.

  • Fear of germs or contamination
  • Unwanted forbidden or taboo thoughts involving sex, religion, and harm
  • Aggressive thoughts towards others or self
  • Having things symmetrical or in a perfect order

Eating Disorder

A severe disturbance in eating behaviours that stem from low self esteem about body weight and shape. Although their are many varieties they all are unhealthy and in extreme cases can lead to death from malnutrition.

  • Extreme flunctuations in weight, from extreme skinniness to obesity.
  • Body dysmorphia and low self confidence
  • Feeling a need for intense control about intake and exercise
  • Exercising compulsively and abusing weight loss supplements and laxatives
